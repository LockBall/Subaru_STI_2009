# Cabin Air Filter — 2009 WRX STI

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16. This file covers the HVAC/cabin filter, not the engine air filter.

| Part | Status | Source |

## Recommended activated-carbon filter

| Part | Fitment evidence | What it provides | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| **FRAM Fresh Breeze `CF10930`** | FRAM cross-references it to Subaru `72880FG000`; Subaru catalogs that OE part for the 2009 STI. | Active-carbon media plus Arm & Hammer baking soda for odor and gas reduction; FRAM claims 98% removal of dust, pollen, and other contaminants. | **Recommended.** Established, conventional pleated construction with carbon—no high-restriction HEPA claim. |
| EPAuto `CP930` | EPAuto calls it a replacement for FRAM `CF10930` and Subaru `72880FG000`. | Activated carbon and soda layers. | **Acceptable lower-cost equivalent.** Same fitment class and concept, but not established as the same manufactured filter. |

FRAM is the preferred option if it is readily available. It is not stocked broadly in the U.S.; use EPAuto `CP930` instead if FRAM is inconvenient or materially more expensive. Both are conventional activated-carbon filters in the same fitment class. Neither is a HEPA filter; that is intentional. Replace annually, or sooner if airflow drops or odors return.

Sources: [FRAM CF10930 specifications and OE cross-reference](https://www.rockauto.com/en/moreinfo.php?pk=7975472), [EPAuto CP930 fitment/cross-reference](https://www.desertcart.in/products/202948684-ep-auto-cp-930-cf-10930-replacement-for-subaru-premium-cabin-air-filter-includes-activated-carbon), [Subaru OE fitment for the 2009 STI](https://www.subarupartsdeal.com/oem-2009-subaru-impreza_wrx-cabin_air_filter.html).

### Interpreting Femota's reported pressure drop

At 100 m³/h (59 CFM), the supplied TTRI page reports 43.4 Pa / 0.17 in. H₂O. That is not unusually restrictive for a clean cabin filter: a published ISO 11155-1 reference-filter table gives an average of 59 Pa at the same 100 m³/h test point. On this limited comparison, Femota's clean-flow resistance is lower than the reference filter, not a blower-risk result. It does **not** predict resistance after dust loading, actual in-car airflow, or the result at maximum blower speed.

Source for the ISO 11155 reference-filter values: [published reference-filter table](https://patents.justia.com/patent/10214079).

## Rejected HEPA + activated-carbon option

| Part | Fitment evidence | What it provides | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PUREFLOW / Premium Guard `PC6115HX`** | PUREFLOW sells this part specifically for the 2009 Impreza; its installation sheet cross-references Subaru `72880-FG000`. | Advertised 99.97%-efficient HEPA stage, activated-charcoal stages with baking soda for gases/odors, plus antibacterial treatment. | **Do not buy.** See failure reports below. |

This is the compatible combined-media option found, but it is **not recommended**. Multiple owner reviews describe its center adhesive releasing, the filter media being drawn into the HVAC blower, and blower seizure or recurring noise afterward. These reports do not establish a failure rate or prove every claimed blower failure, but the consequence is expensive enough that the part is unsuitable for this car. The manufacturer also warns that the HEPA media may cause a minor airflow reduction.

The 99.97% figure is the manufacturer's claim; no independent test report or HEPA certification for this exact automotive part was found, so it should not be described as independently verified “true HEPA.”

Sources: [PUREFLOW 2009 product page](https://pureflowair.com/products/2009-subaru-impreza-cabin-air-filter-hepa-pc6115hx), [PC6115HX installation sheet / OE cross-reference](https://pureflowair.com/cdn/shop/files/PC6115HX.pdf?v=12405165130914075557), [owner-review page containing the media/blower-failure reports](https://www.desertcart.in/products/465428943-pureflow-hepa-cabin-air-filter-pc6115hx-fits-2021-13-subaru).

## Procedure

1. Empty and open the glovebox.
2. Unclip the damper arm on the right side of the glovebox.
3. Squeeze the glovebox sides inward and lower it past the stops.
4. Press the two tabs on the cabin-filter cover and remove the old filter.
5. Remove leaves and debris from the housing without pushing debris farther into the HVAC case.
6. Install the new filter with its airflow arrow pointing the same direction as the old filter. In the AutoZone video, airflow is downward.
7. Refit the cover, raise the glovebox, reattach the damper arm, and check that the glovebox opens and closes normally.

Replace sooner if airflow is weak, odors persist, or the element is visibly loaded with debris.
